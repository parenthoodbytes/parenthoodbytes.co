Episode 22: Erin McKean

Episode Notes

In this episode, we engage in a conversation with Erin McKean, the founder of wordnik.com and a member of the open-source programs office at Google, currently based in Oregon. Erin provides insights into the experience of parenting an adult child, as her son approaches his 24th birthday.
Throughout the discussion, Erin delves into the contrasts between parenting an adult child and a younger one, noting the opportunities for more friendship-like interactions as children mature. Erin highlights the significance of trusting kids and assuming they have things handled and that they will ask for help when they need it.
Furthermore, Erin shares a valuable piece of advice for parents, emphasizing the transient nature of challenges, even though they may appear forever in the moment. She encourages parents to embrace the idea of seeking support when necessary, emphasizing its acceptability and beneficial outcomes.
If you'd like to learn more about Erin's non-profit, wordnik, be sure to check it out at wordnik.com and adopt a word if you'd like!
