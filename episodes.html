Episode 1: Jon

Jon is a Senior Software Engineer, currently working as a Consultant. He has two kids ages 8 and 10. In this episode, he talks about the differences of his first parental leave at his company as well as the things his wife and he do to get things done. His tip for new parents is to be cautious of and do what you can to avoid burnout.

Episode 2: Abdul

Abdul is an IT manager in Ireland, who juggles a demanding career at a large tech company while parenting two little ones, aged 4 and 2. Abdul shares invaluable insights for new parents and fellow parents in tech, urging them not to shy away from discussing parental leave - communication is key. Abdul advocates being open with your manager and team, while also encouraging parents to ask as many questions as they need to feel supported and empowered. He also talks about the importance of taking personal time off - not just for the family but for yourself as well. He highlights the need to avoid the dreaded burnout that so often lurks in the background.

Episode 3: Julie Hollek

In this episode of Parenthood Bytes, we meet Julie Hollek, an inspiring mother and Director of Data Science at Mozilla, currently based in Texas. Julie shares her incredible journey as the primary breadwinner while raising two children, aged 11 and 17 months old, and how she successfully manages the responsibilities in collaboration with her partner.

Julie's story unfolds as she navigates the intricate balance of parenting, professional growth, and her pursuit of knowledge, while raising a toddler and working in the dynamic tech industry. She provides valuable insights and advice for parents, emphasizing the importance of not letting fear hold you back. Julie encourages parents to understand that while there is never a "perfect time" to have kids, with resilience and determination, you can overcome any challenge that comes your way.

Episode 4: Bobby

In this episode, we sit down with Bobby, a Senior Project Manager at Amazon, currently based in Texas. Bobby shares the highs, lows, and the sheer joy of raising his two kids, aged 12 and 10.

Bobby provides a candid look into the challenges and rewards of parenthood, offering his experience and wisdom. His practical tip for fellow parents is: anticipate and savor the significant changes that parenthood brings. While vacationing with kids is entirely possible, Bobby emphasizes the importance of taking vacation time with just your partner.

Furthermore, he advocates for a balanced perspective on work, suggesting that finding a job that prioritizes family can make all the difference. As he aptly puts it, "don't take work so seriously."

Episode 5: Song

This episode features an insightful conversation with Song, the VP of Technical Services at Nium, residing in the Bay Area. With almost 25 years of experience in the tech industry and a 17-year-old son, Song shares valuable perspectives on successful parenting in the tech world. The key lies in effective communication with both your company and your partner.

Song highlights the significance of achieving a balance with your partner when it comes to roles and responsibilities in parenting. He underscores the importance of open and frequent communication, emphasizing that there is no one-size-fits-all blueprint for success. What works for one family may not work for another, and vice versa. The crucial aspect is to identify and implement what is best for your own family dynamics.

Episode 6: Mahima

In this installment, we sit down with Mahima, an Engineering Manager at Spotify, who currently lives in New York. She brings 18 years of tech industry experience to the table. Despite a career-focused trajectory in the earlier part of her professional life, Mahima became a parent just a few years ago, and her one-and-a-half-year-old son has since reshaped her priorities.

Mahima candidly shares how the first decade-plus of her career was predominantly centered around her professional pursuits, but the recent years have seen a deliberate effort to strike a balance between career and personal life.

Offering valuable advice to fellow parents, Mahima stresses the importance of not attempting to juggle everything at once. Recognizing that the initial 1-2 years of parenthood can be challenging, she encourages parents to go easy on themselves and fully embrace parental leave. Mahima advocates for a gradual return to work and advises collaborating with both the team and manager to establish a well-thought-out plan. She reassures parents that returning at full throttle is not the expectation, and self-compassion is key during this transitional period. Tune in for Mahima's insights on navigating the complexities of parenthood and career in the tech world.

Episode 7: Will Ramirez

In this episode, we engage in a conversation with Will Ramirez, a Senior Software Engineer at Mongo DB, currently residing in Florida. Will generously shares his experiences of raising a 6-year-old and a 17-year-old, offering valuable insights for parents navigating the challenges of family life.

Will's advice serves as a universal truth for all parents: no matter how meticulously you plan your life, having kids introduces an element of unpredictability. Drawing a parallel to planning in the workplace, Will highlights that just as one plans for the future in their professional life, unexpected events and changes are inevitable. Parenting, like work planning, requires adaptability and flexibility as circumstances evolve.

Tune in to gain perspective from Will's journey, discovering how adaptability and a resilient mindset play crucial roles in successfully navigating the dynamic landscape of parenthood.

Episode 8: Ingrid Yen

In this episode, we engage in a conversation with Ingrid Yen, a Staff Technical Program Manager at Github, who shares her insights and experiences of raising children, particularly at the ages of 6 and 12. One of the focus of the discussion revolves around the transition of kids to owning cell phones and the measures Ingrid has taken to both protect her children and educate them about responsible phone usage.

Ingrid recommends valuable resources for parents and kids, including documentaries like "The Social Dilemma," "Childhood 2.0," and "Screenagers," which shed light on the impact of technology on children's lives.

One of Ingrid's key pieces of advice for parents is to timebox activities, ensuring that dedicated time is set aside for top priorities, with a strong emphasis on spending quality time with children. She emphasizes the importance of involving kids in activities such as cooking and baking, not only as a means to teach them essential life skills but also as an opportunity to bond and create lasting memories.

Overall, the episode offers practical insights into managing the challenges of parenting in the digital age, with a focus on balancing technology use, setting boundaries, and fostering meaningful connections with children through shared activities.

Episode 9: Pedro Rico

In this episode, we sit down with Pedro Rico, a seasoned Staff Software Engineer in the music streaming industry with 16-17 years of experience. Alongside his tech career, Pedro is also a dedicated parent to a 6-year-old son.

Pedro candidly shares his most significant parental challenge—navigating the delicate balance between ambitious career goals and family life. Pedro talks about how he manages to juggle both aspects of his life while striving to maintain a healthy work-life balance.

Pedro offers a practical tip for those contemplating parenthood. He suggests that if you have a penchant for travel or enjoy adult-oriented experiences such as museum visits, it's advisable to indulge in these before starting a family. However, for parents who embark on adventures with young kids, Pedro provides a checklist of essential items for a smooth journey, including snacks, extra diapers, a car seat, items to help children adjust their ears, and of course extra clothing for you and your children!

Tune in to hear more about Pedro's experiences and struggles managing life as a parent and as a parent in tech.

Episode 10: Adam Cando

In this episode, we engage in a captivating conversation with Adam Cando, a seasoned software engineering manager at Loyal, with over 13 years of experience in the tech industry, and is currently raising a 4-year-old.

Adam reflects on the advantageous aspects of a tech career, emphasizing the flexibility it affords, including accommodating work hours and the ability to be present for his child whenever necessary. A unique aspect of his parenting approach is highlighted as he discusses the practice of gifting experiences rather than physical items to his daughter. He shares how this strategy has accelerated her learning and development, setting her on a unique path compared to her peers. Adam encourages parents to incorporate their passions into their children's upbringing, creating a more enriching and fulfilling family dynamic.

Tune in to this episode for a blend of professional wisdom and parenting insights, as Adam shares his experiences, offering valuable tips for those navigating the intersection of a demanding tech career and the joys of parenthood.

Episode 11: Bernadette Castaneda

In this episode, we chat with Bernadette, a Director of Risk who brings a unique perspective to the table. Despite spending the majority of her career outside the tech industry, she has embraced the challenges and opportunities of the field for the past 7 years.

Bernadette shares the distinctive challenges of balancing work and family life, particularly the perpetual feeling that more could be done on both fronts. However, she offers a reassuring insight—nobody expects perfection all the time. Bernadette reflects on the struggle of accepting that it's okay for both work and family to have their demands without the expectation of always giving 100%.

A significant aspect of the discussion revolves around Bernadette's experience as a parent to a 24-year-old son. She delves into the complexities of allowing him to make his own mistakes and learn from them, acknowledging the inherent difficulty in stepping back and letting the learning process unfold naturally.

Bernadette provides a practical tip on maintaining a healthy work-life balance. She emphasizes the importance of intentional disconnection when taking time away from work. By setting clear boundaries and being fully present during family time, she believes it's possible to create a more fulfilling and harmonious balance between professional and personal life.
